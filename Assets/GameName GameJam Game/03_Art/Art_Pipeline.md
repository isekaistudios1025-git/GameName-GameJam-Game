## Overview

The art pipeline defines how visual assets are created and integrated into the game.

It ensures consistency, clarity, and efficiency for all art production.

---

## Core Principle

> Art must support gameplay first.

All assets should prioritize:

- Readability
- Clarity
- Animation usefulness

---

## Character Asset Requirements

Each character (player or enemy) requires:

- Idle
- Movement (walk/run)
- Attack
- Hit reaction

Optional:
- Death animation
- Special attack

---

## Animation Guidelines

- Keep animation frame count LOW
- Focus on clear silhouettes
- Exaggerate motion for readability
- Prioritize timing over detail

---

## Sprite Sheet Requirements

- Consistent frame size
- Aligned pivot points
- Even spacing between frames

---

## Style Direction

- Inspired by classic arcade beat 'em ups
- Bold shapes and readable silhouettes
- Avoid overly detailed designs

---

## Production Workflow

1. Concept art created
2. Design approved
3. Sprite sheet created
4. Animation frames finalized
5. Imported into Unity
6. Tested in gameplay

---

## Communication Rules

Avoid vague direction like:
- “be creative”

Instead define:
- What the character does
- What animations are required
- What gameplay role they serve

---

## Scope Constraints

- Limit number of characters
- Reuse animations where possible
- Avoid over-detailing assets

---

## Notes

> “Readable animation beats detailed art every time.”
